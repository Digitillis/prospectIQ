# ProspectIQ — 2-Week GTM Game Plan

> **Goal**: Maximum traction by Day 15. At least 2-3 warm conversations with potential pilot customers, 200+ qualified prospects ready for outreach, and perfected messaging.
> **Constraint**: Cold email warmup completes ~Day 14. LinkedIn and direct channels available immediately.
> **Owner**: Avanish Mehrotra

---

## The Math

To get 2-3 pilot conversations by Day 30, you need (working backwards):
- **2-3 demos booked** (requires ~50 contacted, at 5% booking rate)
- **50 enriched contacts with emails** (requires ~80 qualified)
- **80 qualified** (requires ~200 researched, at 40% qualification rate)
- **200 researched** (requires ~500 discovered, costs ~$10 in API)
- **500 discovered** (3-4 discovery runs, free)

Plus parallel channel: **20-30 LinkedIn DMs** (10-15% response rate = 2-4 conversations)

## Week 1: Build the Arsenal (Days 1-7)

### Day 1-2: Pipeline Loading (3 hours/day)

**ProspectIQ Pipeline Runs:**
- [ ] Run Discovery: F&B tiers fb1-fb5, 5 pages each → ~500 prospects
- [ ] Run Discovery: Discrete mfg tiers mfg1-mfg2, 3 pages each → ~300 prospects
- [ ] Run Research on top 50 F&B prospects (cost: ~$2.50)
- [ ] Run Qualification on all researched
- [ ] Run Enrichment on top 25 qualified F&B contacts

**Target**: 800 discovered, 50 researched, 30+ qualified, 25 enriched

### Day 2-3: Perfect the Message (2 hours/day)

**Outreach Guidelines Iteration:**
- [ ] Run Outreach on 10 qualified F&B companies → generates 10 drafts
- [ ] "Send Test to Me" on all 10 → review in your inbox
- [ ] For each draft, note what feels wrong (too formal? too generic? wrong angle?)
- [ ] Update Outreach Guidelines on Settings page based on feedback
- [ ] Re-run Outreach on 10 different companies → compare quality
- [ ] Repeat until drafts feel like YOU wrote them
- [ ] Run Outreach on 5 discrete mfg companies → verify PdM angle works too

**Quality bar**: Every draft should be something you'd proudly send with zero edits.

### Day 3-4: LinkedIn Activation (1.5 hours/day)

**This is your immediate outreach channel — no warmup needed.**

- [ ] Update LinkedIn headline: "Founder & CEO @ Digitillis | AI for Manufacturing & Food Safety"
- [ ] Write and post 3 LinkedIn articles (schedule across the week):
  1. "The real cost of FSMA non-compliance for mid-market food manufacturers" (data-driven)
  2. "Why predictive maintenance fails at most manufacturers (and what actually works)" (contrarian take)
  3. "We trained AI on NASA turbofan data. Here's what it taught us about equipment failure." (technical credibility)
- [ ] Send 10 LinkedIn connection requests/day to F&B VP Quality / Food Safety contacts from your prospect list
  - Use the enriched contact data from ProspectIQ (you have LinkedIn URLs)
  - Connection note: Short, specific to their company, no pitch. Just connect.
  - Example: "Hi [Name], I noticed [Company] is in [meat/dairy/bakery]. I'm working on AI compliance tools for food manufacturers — would love to connect."
- [ ] Comment meaningfully on 5 manufacturing/food safety posts per day
  - Add genuine insight, not "Great post!" — share a data point or contrarian view
  - Target posts from: Food Safety Magazine, IFSQN, SQF Institute, Manufacturing Leadership Council

## Week 2: Start Conversations (Days 8-14)

### Day 8-9: LinkedIn Follow-Up + Warm Outreach (2 hours/day)

- [ ] For accepted LinkedIn connections: Send a follow-up message (NOT a pitch)
  ```
  Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Quick question — how are you handling
  CCP documentation for your [product] line? Curious if you've seen
  any good solutions or if it's still mostly manual.
  ```
  This opens a conversation without pitching. If they respond, THEN share what you're building.
- [ ] Post 2 more LinkedIn articles:
  4. "3 questions every food safety VP should ask before their next FDA audit"
  5. "The 18-day warning: How AI predicts equipment failures before they happen"
- [ ] Continue 10 connection requests/day + 5 meaningful comments/day
- [ ] Follow up with any food safety consultant responses — book 15-min calls

### Day 9-10: Warm Intro Blitz (2 hours)

**Map your existing network for manufacturing connections:**
- [ ] Go through your LinkedIn connections (all of them)
- [ ] Identify anyone who:
  - Works in manufacturing operations
  - Knows someone who runs a food plant
  - Is a management consultant who serves manufacturers
  - Works at a manufacturing technology company
- [ ] Send 15-20 personal messages asking for intros:
  ```
  Hey [Name], I'm launching Digitillis — an AI platform for manufacturers.
  We predict equipment failures and automate food safety compliance.
  Do you know anyone who runs operations at a food manufacturer or
  mid-size machining shop? Would love a warm intro if so.
  ```
- [ ] Track every intro in ProspectIQ

### Day 10-11: Content & Credibility (2 hours)

- [ ] Write and publish 1 blog post on digitillis.com:
  "FSMA Compliance Checklist: 5 Documentation Gaps That Trigger 483 Observations"
  (This is your gated content — add email capture form)
- [ ] Create a 1-page PDF:
  "Digitillis for Food Manufacturers — Continuous CCP Monitoring & FSMA Documentation"
  (Use the one-pager content from docs/commercial/gtm/ONE_PAGER_FB.md)
- [ ] Post the blog on LinkedIn with a compelling hook

### Day 11-12: Pipeline Scaling (2 hours)

- [ ] Run Discovery with new keyword sets:
  - "frozen food", "snack food", "pet food" (expand F&B)
  - "USDA inspected", "FDA registered" (compliance-focused companies)
- [ ] Research top 30 from new discovery
- [ ] Qualify → Enrich → Generate outreach drafts
- [ ] Final messaging review — reject anything below quality bar

### Day 13: Pre-Launch Check (1 hour)

- [ ] Verify Instantly.ai warmup status — should show "Ready" or "Warmed"
- [ ] Review all approved drafts one final time
- [ ] Check suppression list — no duplicates, no bounced domains
- [ ] Verify Resend sender domain (avi@digitillis.com) is authenticated
- [ ] Test end-to-end: Approve a draft → Run Engagement → verify it appears in Instantly

### Day 14: Launch Day (2 hours)

- [ ] Run Engagement (send_approved) — sends all approved drafts via Instantly.ai
- [ ] Start with 10-15 emails on Day 1 (don't blast 200 at once — ramp gradually)
- [ ] Monitor opens/clicks in Instantly.ai dashboard
- [ ] Set up the daily routine:
  - Morning: Check Instantly for opens/replies, check ProspectIQ for hot signals
  - Midday: Run buying signal detection, process any follow-up sequences
  - Evening: Generate 5-10 new outreach drafts for the next day

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't build more features**. The platform is built. Sell it.
- **Don't blast 500 emails on Day 14**. Ramp gradually: 10 → 20 → 30 → 50/day.
- **Don't pitch on LinkedIn connection requests**. Connect first, converse second, pitch never (let them ask).
- **Don't spend time on discrete mfg in Week 1**. F&B first — it's your strongest angle.
- **Don't wait for perfect messaging**. Good enough + sent > perfect + sitting in drafts.
- **Don't ignore food safety consultants**. One warm intro from a trusted advisor is worth 50 cold emails.
- **Don't create a pitch deck**. Nobody reads decks. The demo video and 1-pager are enough.

## The One Thing That Matters Most

If you do nothing else from this plan, do this:

**Send 10 LinkedIn DMs per day to VP Food Safety / VP Quality contacts at mid-market food manufacturers.**

Not a pitch. A genuine question about their CCP documentation process. The conversations that start from these DMs will become your first pilot customers. Everything else is support infrastructure for this one activity.
