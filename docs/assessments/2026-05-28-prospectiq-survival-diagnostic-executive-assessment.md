BOARD ASSESSMENT — PROSPECTIQ, 2026-05-28

VERDICT: Partial rebuild (Option C). ProspectIQ stays a COLD-outbound channel — that decision is settled and not in question. Do not retire it — the sourcing, drafting, delivery, and bounce-governance mechanics work and the best 15-20% of emails are good enough to send today. Do not resume at volume — more sends makes the result strictly worse. Rebuild the personalization, sequence, qualification, and measurement layers so every email is genuinely 1:1 personalized to the individual, then resume on a tighter, higher-specificity footprint.

NOTE ON SCOPE (revised 2026-05-28): an earlier draft of this assessment recommended pivoting toward a warm-outbound, FirstLook-anchored motion. That recommendation is withdrawn at the founder's direction. The channel is cold outbound; the lever is per-individual personalization plus platform remediation. All recommendations below are scoped to that decision. The detailed engineering remediations live in the companion Platform Remediation Plan.

WHAT THE HEADLINE DOES AND DOES NOT MEAN. "1,250 sent, ~0 replies" is real but it is not a finished-campaign demand verdict. 89% of sends were intentionally-soft Step-1 curiosity openers; the value prop and the ask live in Steps 3+, which were effectively never sent (8 sends total). The honest baseline is 880 Step-1 touches to one reply (0.11%) against a realistic personalized Step-1 floor of 0.3-0.7%. So the result is anomalously low but not catastrophic on volume alone. The founder's caveat is valid — and it is also partially undercut by the data. The 94% click-to-open ratio (industry norm 10-25%) and fully co-registered open/click events mean most "engagement" is security-scanner pixel loads, not humans; genuine human reads are likely ~20-30, not the 53 reported. So "0 replies" sits on a funnel with far fewer real readers than the raw numbers imply. Both things are true at once.

THE FOUR HONEST CONCLUSIONS.

1) Targeting is mostly sound and is NOT the problem. 77.9% of contacts carry the decision-maker flag; Plant Manager, COO, and VP Operations are 64.8% of sends — exactly the buyer. The alarming "239 non-manufacturer contacts" is a labeling bug (Tsubaki Nakashima, Dezurik, GKN Powder Metallurgy are real manufacturers mislabeled non_mfg); true contamination is ~30-50 companies (4-6%). Stop treating list contamination as the headline failure. It is a rounding error.

2) Message quality is a real binding constraint — for the bulk, not the best. The corpus is genuinely bimodal. The top 15-20% (Waupaca induction furnace, AMETEK VAR furnace, ECMS/BASF carve-out, Sekisui/Boeing) are asset-specific, event-hooked, single-question — and the one genuine reply came from exactly this category. The bottom 60-65% are interchangeable templates: "time-based maintenance" appears in 35% of all emails, "typically" in 56%, and three recycled stat ranges (15-20% / 23-41% / 40-65%) are sprayed identically across foundries, dairies, and aerospace, with "18 days out" repeated verbatim across asset classes it cannot apply to uniformly. To any VP Ops who has seen modern SDR email, that bulk is recognizable as machine output in five seconds. The problem is not the soft CTA (correct for Step 1); it is the templated middle.

3) The autonomy split is inverted, and verified in code. The system runs autonomous draft generation every 5 minutes and autonomous keyword qualification every 15 minutes, then gates sending behind a per-email human review that passed 99.3% of 1,250 emails — eight genuine rejections were the entire yield. That is a rubber stamp consuming the founder's scarcest resource while filtering nothing, and it manufactures false confidence (it feels like quality control while the templated 65% sails through). Worse, 46 already-rejected drafts were sent anyway. The correct split is the exact opposite: automate research, enrichment, and draft prep behind an enforced per-individual personalization gate; put the human at the two points that matter — the upstream ICP/list decision and the reply handoff — not at serial per-email approval.

4) Reply capture is broken and already cost a live opportunity. Verified in code: the IMAP cron searches UNSEEN-only (gmail_imap.py:175). Because the founder reads his own inbox first, the system has ingested zero replies via IMAP, ever. The only warm prospect in the entire dataset — Nathan Thayne at Waupaca Foundry — sent two replies; the second was an explicit "call whenever" scheduling invitation that has no record anywhere in the database. The only company with a confirmed warm reply is not even marked engaged, while five companies were promoted to "engaged" on scanner clicks. There is no CRM (no tables exist). This is a genuine correctness failure that lost a real opportunity — but it is downstream of the actual growth problem.

THE STRATEGIC ERROR. ProspectIQ is built as a volume email machine: Apollo pull, AI drafting at scale, serial approval, bulk send. The right variable for cold outbound to plant managers is not volume — it is per-individual specificity. The system already proves this internally: the one reply came from the one email written as if a human had spent twenty minutes on the company (Waupaca's induction-furnace coil/refractory failure mode, in correct foundry vocabulary). The defect is that the system can produce that email but does not REQUIRE it — 60-65% of sends are interchangeable templates that any of the 887 companies could have received unchanged. The correction is to make the Waupaca standard mandatory: every email must hook on a verifiable, company- or person-specific fact (a named event, a named asset, a role-specific detail), or it does not send. A signal/intent layer (new Maintenance Director hire, CMMS/ERP replacement, capex, reliability-engineer postings — which every 2026 competitor runs and ProspectIQ lacks) belongs here too, but as cold-send timing and personalization input, not as a channel change. The wedge is not "more AI cold email" and it is not a warm motion — it is genuinely 1:1 cold email at the specificity the best 15-20% already demonstrate.

WHY NOT SCALE. The AI-SDR category proved full-autonomy-at-volume is operationally toxic: deliverability falls from 90%+ to under 60% by week four, 50-70% annual churn, $150-300 per meeting once reputation degrades. 783-to-0 was not a volume deficit. 7,830 sends multiplies the templated 65%, burns bounce budget against the 2% ceiling, and destroys sender reputation. The math runs opposite to intuition: a 100-200 account, deeply-personalized, fully-sequenced cold campaign will out-produce a 78,300 cold blast.

WHY NOT RETIRE. The hard mechanics — Apollo sourcing, draft generation, Resend delivery (94.3%), the bounce gate (verified: assert_bounce_rate_ok defaults to send_path and raises to block sends above 2% — the May 25 "is it enforcing?" doubt is closed in code, docs just never caught up), the JSON-parse fix, the atomic queue claim — are real assets. Throwing them away to rebuild plumbing would be waste. The defect is in the layers above the plumbing.

THE ORGANIZATIONAL ROOT. The timeline shows a chronic loop: every defect is diagnosed, declared FIXED, and rediscovered 1-2 weeks later (bounce gate, reply tracking, email_status nulls, hallucinations, approved_by fields, pacing config). Documentation grew to 50+ files while commercial outcomes never moved. Fixes land at the symptom layer; root causes (list signal quality, the unconstrained generation space, the missing GTM theory) go untouched. The 90-day plan must break this loop with hard kill-criteria, not another doctrine document.

BOTTOM LINE FOR THE BOARD. This is a misaimed-but-salvageable asset, not a failed one, and it stays a cold-outbound channel. Stop building the volume machine. Fix reply capture, consolidate to one or two warmed cold domains, make the Waupaca-style asset-specific email the enforced standard (no specific hook, no send), deepen enrichment so that specificity is feasible at scale, fix qualification and sequence completion, and replace per-email rubber-stamp review with an automated personalization gate plus sampled QA. Prove it on 100-200 deeply-personalized, fully-sequenced cold accounts before spending another engineering week on volume. If that cohort does not produce qualified conversations on a fair test, the verdict flips to retire-outbound and reallocate to phone/referral. We will know inside 90 days. The prescriptive engineering steps are in the companion Platform Remediation Plan.
