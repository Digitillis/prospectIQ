# ProspectIQ Survival Diagnostic — Strategic Recommendation

*Date: 2026-05-28  |  Verdict: C — Partial Rebuild  |  Confidence: HIGH*

*Author: Avanish Mehrotra & Digitillis Technical Team*

*Revised 2026-05-28: channel decision fixed to COLD outbound. The prior warm/FirstLook-anchored recommendation is withdrawn at founder direction.*

---

RECOMMENDED FUTURE STATE: ProspectIQ remains a **cold-outbound** channel. The correction is to make every email genuinely 1:1 personalized to the individual recipient — the standard the best 15-20% of the current corpus already demonstrate — and to remediate the platform defects that suppress and mis-measure outcomes. The channel is not the problem and is not changing. Volume is not the lever; per-individual specificity is.

WHY:
- The audience is right. Targeting is sound (77.9% decision-makers; Plant Manager / COO / VP Ops = 64.8% of sends). The failure is message specificity in the bulk, channel hygiene, and broken measurement — not who is being contacted.
- The system already proves the thesis internally. The single confirmed reply (Waupaca Foundry) came from the one email that hooked on a verifiable, asset-specific failure mode in correct foundry vocabulary. AMETEK's VAR-furnace email and the APP NADCAP-casting email are the same caliber. The capability exists; it is simply not required of every send.
- The bulk is the problem. 60-65% of emails are interchangeable templates — "time-based maintenance" in 35%, "typically" in 56%, three recycled stat ranges (15-20% / 23-41% / 40-65%) sprayed across foundries, dairies, and aerospace, "18 days out" verbatim across asset classes it cannot uniformly apply to. Experienced buyers recognize this as machine output in five seconds and delete it. This is a real reply-suppressor independent of the (correct) soft Step-1 CTA.
- Personalization at this depth is impossible without data. The EnrichmentAgent has been paused since 2026-05-07 and 95% of "researched" companies carry no research_intelligence record. You cannot write the Waupaca email for 1,000 contacts without per-individual enrichment feeding it. Enrichment depth is therefore a precondition, not a nice-to-have.
- Volume is strictly the wrong lever. The AI-SDR collapse (11x / Artisan: deliverability 90% to under 60% by week 4, 50-70% churn, $150-300/meeting) proves scaling the current templated motion destroys sender reputation. A smaller, deeply-personalized, fully-sequenced cold campaign out-produces a larger templated blast.

EXPECTED IMPACT:
- A cold campaign where every email meets the Waupaca specificity bar, sent to a tightly-qualified set of high-fit accounts and fully sequenced (not abandoned at Step 1), should lift the Step-1 reply rate from ~0.1% toward the 0.3-0.7%+ realistic floor for well-personalized manufacturing cold email, and — the only metric that matters at this stage — produce qualified manufacturing conversations.
- Founder time per qualified conversation drops sharply because the serial per-email rubber stamp (99.3% pass, 8 rejections ever) is replaced by an automated personalization gate plus sampled QA, with human judgment moved upstream to ICP/list approval and downstream to reply handoff.

RISKS:
- Hypothetical: even with perfect per-individual personalization, this buyer may transact poorly via cold email — manufacturing buying cycles run 12-24 months and the persona skews phone/peer-referral. The 90-day plan must carry a hard kill-criterion that flips the verdict to "retire outbound, move to phone/referral/events" if a fair, fully-personalized cohort fails.
- Organizational risk (evidenced, not hypothetical): the diagnose-claim-rediscover loop will reassert unless every remediation lands with a regression test and binary kill-criteria, and unless the team stops producing process documents in place of fixes.

WHAT NOT TO DO: Do not change the channel. Do not introduce a warm-outbound or FirstLook-anchored motion (explicitly out of scope per founder direction). Do not add more volume, more autonomy, or more un-grounded AI. Do not rebuild the working plumbing (Apollo sourcing, Resend delivery, bounce gate, atomic queue claim). Do not write another doctrine document — the corpus already shows the team out-documents its outcomes; the fix is execution against the Platform Remediation Plan and its measurable gates.
